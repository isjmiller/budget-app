Step 1: Income — What jobs will you have?
  • Job 1 (2025–2026) — A starter job you can get now: barista, server, retail, daycare worker, etc.
  • Job 2 (2027–2029) — Something related to your career: internship, entry-level, or trade apprenticeship
  • Job 3 (2030+) — Your actual career job — the one you're going to school for
  • Look up realistic salaries, then hit Auto-Sync — your net pay fills into the Budget automatically
💡 Resources: Paycheck Calculator · Bureau of Labor Statistics (salary lookup) · Google "[your career] average salary [your state]"

🏠 Next: Housing (Step 3) — Find places to live for 2025, 2027, 2030, and 2040 →
3
Step 3: Housing — Where will you live?
  • 2025 — Dorm, with family, or splitting with roommates right out of high school (could be $0)
  • 2027 — An apartment shared with 2–4 roommates to keep costs low
  • 2030 — A place to live on your own or with a partner
  • 2040 — A house you might want to buy someday
  • Enter the monthly rent/mortgage. The app splits costs by number of roommates automatically.
💡 Resources: Zillow.com · Apartments.com · Redfin.com

Step 4: Auto — What cars will you drive?

💰 Next: Retirement (Step 7) — Set up your retirement savings plan →
7
Step 7: Retirement — Start saving early!
  • Set Current Age to how old you'll be in 2030 (when your career job starts)
  • Set Retirement Age to 67
  • Set Expected Annual Return to 5.75%
  • Set Annual Contribution to roughly 10% of your net income from your career job
  • Watch how compound interest turns small monthly contributions into a huge nest egg!
💡 Even saving $200/month starting at age 22 can grow to over $700,000 by retirement at a 7% return.
